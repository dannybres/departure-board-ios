# Departure Board — Marketing Copy

---

## Short (one paragraph)

Departure Board is the fastest way to check live UK train times on your iPhone or iPad. Tap any station and instantly see real-time departures and arrivals, platform numbers, delay reasons, and full calling-point timelines — all pulled straight from National Rail. Add your regular stations as favourites, put a live board on your Home Screen with a widget, and Departure Board can even open the right board the moment you launch it based on your location. Whether you're running for a train or planning your commute, it's the one app you'll actually want to check.

---

## Medium (with lists)

Departure Board puts the full power of National Rail live data in your pocket. In seconds you can pull up any UK station's departures or arrivals, see exactly which platform your train is at, check whether it's on time, and drill into every calling point along the route. It's the departure board on the wall — but smarter, always with you, and entirely on your terms.

**What you get:**

- **Live boards for every UK station** — departures and arrivals, sorted by time, with platform badges, delay warnings, and cancellation alerts at a glance
- **Full service detail** — tap any train to see its complete calling-point timeline, scheduled vs. expected times, coach loading percentages, and a live map of the route
- **Favourites & shortcuts** — star your regular stations (e.g. London Waterloo departures, or Manchester Piccadilly arrivals from Liverpool) and they appear as Home Screen quick actions with one tap
- **Filtered boards** — travelling to Clapham Junction from Waterloo? Filter the board to show only trains calling there, then save that filter as a favourite
- **Home Screen widgets** — small, medium, or large, showing one or two stations' live departures right on your Home Screen, updating every 5 minutes
- **Auto-launch on arrival** — set the app to automatically open your nearest station's board (or your nearest favourite) the moment you launch it
- **Station information** — address, facilities, ticket office hours, accessibility details, and opening hours all in one place
- **Split service support** — trains that divide mid-route are shown clearly, with separate calling-point lists and map branches for each portion
- **Deep links & Shortcuts** — open any board from the Shortcuts app, Safari bookmarks, or another app using `departure://departures/WAT`

Departure Board is built for the way people actually travel — fast checks on the platform, widgets on the lock screen, and smart defaults that get you to the right information without digging through menus.

## Long (all features)

### The only departure board app you need

Departure Board is a native iPhone and iPad app that gives you real-time UK train departures and arrivals for every station on the National Rail network. It's built around a simple idea: your time matters, and checking train times should take seconds, not taps.

---

### Live boards — real data, no guessing

Open any of the 2,000+ UK stations and you get a live departure or arrivals board, exactly like the one at the station — but on your phone, before you've even left the house. Every service shows:

- **Scheduled and expected times**, colour-coded so you can see at a glance what's on time (white), delayed (orange), or cancelled (red)
- **Platform numbers** displayed as badges, with a special BUS label for rail replacement coaches
- **Cancellation and delay reasons** — not just a red X, but the actual reason National Rail is reporting
- **NRCC network messages** — disruption notices from National Rail Control Centre shown at the top of the board
- **Buses alongside trains** — rail replacement services shown in a separate section so nothing gets missed

Switch between departures and arrivals with a single tap. Everything updates automatically every 60 seconds, and you can pull to refresh any time.

---

### Service detail — the full picture

Tap any service and you get everything. Departure Board doesn't just show you the next stop — it shows you the entire journey:

- A **visual calling-point timeline** with colour-coded indicators: grey for future stops, blue for the current stop, green for stops already passed, red for cancelled stops
- **Scheduled, expected, and actual times** at every calling point, so you can see if a train is recovering time or falling further behind
- **Platform information** at each stop along the route
- **Cancellation and delay reasons** per individual calling point
- A **live map** of the route using Apple Maps, with coloured markers for each stop and polylines tracing the path — including separate branches if the service splits
- **Coach formation details**: carriage numbers, First vs. Standard class, passenger loading shown as a percentage with a colour bar (green under a third full, orange in the middle, red when packed), and toilet availability per coach

For services that split into two portions mid-journey — common on routes like the Brighton Main Line or services to/from the North — Departure Board shows each branch separately with its own calling-point list and its own line on the map.

---

### Filtered boards — cut to what matters

Commuting from Clapham Junction to London Bridge? You don't need to scroll past every service on the board. Departure Board lets you filter any station's board by destination (for departures) or origin (for arrivals):

- Filter departures at **London Waterloo** to show only trains **calling at Woking**
- Filter arrivals at **Manchester Piccadilly** to show only trains **from Liverpool Lime Street**
- Save any filtered board as a **favourite** so it's always one tap away
- Recent filters are remembered automatically and shown at the top of the filter picker
- An active filter is shown as a chip in the board view — tap it to clear instantly

---

### Favourites and shortcuts

Star any board — whether it's a plain station or a filtered view — and it becomes a favourite. Your favourites:

- Appear at the top of the station list for instant access
- Sync to **Home Screen quick actions** — hold the app icon and jump straight to your most-used boards (up to 4)
- Can optionally show the **next departure time** right in the favourites list, so you know your train situation without even opening a board
- Can be reordered to match the priority of your travel patterns

---

### Home Screen widgets

Put a live board right on your Home Screen or Lock Screen. Departure Board includes fully configurable widgets in multiple sizes:

**Single-station widget** (Small, Medium, Large)
- Small: compact time-only view, colour-coded status, fits in a corner of your Home Screen
- Medium: up to 4 services with status
- Large: up to 8 services with full detail

**Dual-station widget** (Medium, Large)
- Show two stations side by side — perfect for seeing both your departure station and your arrival station at once
- Each station is independently configurable with optional filters

All widgets:
- Update every 5 minutes
- Support filtered boards (e.g. show only Paddington → Reading)
- Deep-link back into the app — tap a service to open its full detail view
- Display platform numbers, delay indicators, and cancellation alerts
- Show a BUS icon for rail replacement coaches

---

### Auto-launch on arrival

Departure Board can open the right board the moment you launch the app — no searching required. Choose from four modes in Settings:

- **Disabled** — always shows the full station list (default)
- **Nearest Station** — uses your location to open the closest station's departures automatically
- **Nearby Favourite** — if you're within a set distance (1–50 miles) of a favourite station, it opens that instead
- **Favourite, then Nearest** — checks your favourites first in priority order, falls back to the nearest station if none are close

This means arriving at Euston and opening the app just works — it's already showing your platform.

---

### Station information

Every station has a full information sheet, accessible from the board or the station list. It includes:

- Address and postcode
- Train operating company
- Ticket office hours and staffing levels
- Ticket machines and accepted payment methods
- Accessibility facilities
- Toilet and waiting room availability
- Customer service contact details
- CCTV information
- Left luggage availability
- Travelcard zones (for London stations)
- Opening hours
- Links to Apple Maps or Google Maps (your choice)

---

### Deep links and Shortcuts app integration

Power users can build automations around Departure Board. Every board has a URL scheme:

- `departure://departures/WAT` — open Waterloo departures
- `departure://arrivals/MAN` — open Manchester Piccadilly arrivals
- `departure://departures/WAT?filter=CLJ&filterType=to` — Waterloo departures calling at Clapham Junction
- `departure://service/WAT/SERVICE_ID` — jump directly to a specific service

Use these in:
- **Apple Shortcuts** — trigger a board from a shortcut, a widget, or an automation
- **Safari bookmarks** — one tap from Safari to the live board
- **Third-party apps** — link into Departure Board from any app that supports URL schemes

The Settings screen includes a built-in URL scheme reference with live examples you can copy or open directly.

---

### Design and reliability

Departure Board is built entirely in SwiftUI, designed specifically for iPhone and iPad:

- **iPad support** with split-view list selection for comfortable tablet browsing
- **Dark mode** with properly adjusted platform badges, status colours, and map markers
- **Monospaced time fonts** so columns align cleanly even when scrolling fast
- **Haptic feedback** on key actions — switching board types, toggling favourites, refreshing
- **Automatic 60-second refresh** on boards and service detail, with a live "last updated" counter in the toolbar
- **Offline resilience** — station data is cached locally and only re-fetched when more than 24 hours old

Whether you travel once a week or twice a day, Departure Board is built to be the app that's already showing you the right thing before you've thought to ask.
